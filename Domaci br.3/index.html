Counter-Strike: Global Offensive

Counter-Strike: Global Offensive (CS:GO) is a multiplayer first-person shooter video game developed by Valve and Hidden Path Entertainment. It is the fourth game in the Counter-Strike series and was released for Windows, OS X, Xbox 360, and PlayStation 3 in August 2012, while the Linux version was released in 2014.

Our maps
Counter Strike Global Offensive Maps

Dust II

Dust II is a bomb defusal map in Counter-Strike: Global Offensive. The Counter-Terrorists have two objectives: to prevent the Terrorists from planting the C-4 at either of the two chemical weapon stashes

Inferno

This version of the map has a few visual improvements but not much has changed. The hay stacks and the fish ponds still make an appearance but nuclear containment barrels have also been added.

Mirage

Valve and Hidden Path Entertainment had refined the map's visuals and implemented small gameplay tweaks. For instance, the original versions of Mirage had textures similar to Italy.

Cache

Cache is a bomb defusal map featured in Operation Bravo and was created by FMPONE, Volcano and penE. As of March 2019 it is no longer in the Active Duty pool, pending FMPONE's rework.

Nuke

Nuke is one of Counter-Strike's most iconic and enduring maps. First released in November 1999, the map has undergone numerous aesthetic and functional changes over the years.

Train

Counter-Terrorists: Prevent Terrorists from bombing the nuclear payloads on two trains. Team members must defuse any bombs that threaten the payloads will been remove.
